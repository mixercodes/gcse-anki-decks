Basic	What is the difference between prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells?	Eukaryotic cells (animal/plant) have a true membrane-bound nucleus. Prokaryotic cells (bacteria) have no nucleus — DNA is a single circular strand in the cytoplasm. Prokaryotes have no mitochondria and are much smaller (1–10μm vs 10–100μm). Basic	What is mitosis used for?	Mitosis produces two genetically identical daughter cells with the same number of chromosomes as the parent. Used for growth, repair and asexual reproduction. DNA replicates before division so both cells receive a complete copy.	Science::Biology::Paper 1::Cell Biology
Basic	What are stem cells and why are they medically important?	Undifferentiated cells that can divide and specialise into many cell types. Embryonic stem cells can become any cell type. Adult stem cells (e.g. bone marrow) are more limited. Used to treat leukaemia (bone marrow transplant) and potentially Parkinson's and diabetes. Therapeutic cloning prevents rejection.	Science::Biology::Paper 1::Cell Biology
Basic	What is the difference between diffusion, osmosis and active transport?	Diffusion: passive movement of particles from high to low concentration. Osmosis: passive movement of water molecules across a partially permeable membrane from high to low water potential. Active transport: movement of substances against a concentration gradient using energy (ATP) from respiration — e.g. uptake of mineral ions by root hair cells.	Science::Biology::Paper 1::Cell Biology
