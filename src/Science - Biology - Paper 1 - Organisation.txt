Cloze	Levels of organisation (smallest to largest): {{c1::cell}} → {{c2::tissue}} → {{c3::organ}} → {{c4::organ system}} → {{c5::organism}}.		Science::Biology::Paper 1::Organisation
Basic	What are the four main tissue types in animals?	Epithelial tissue (covers surfaces), muscle tissue (contracts for movement), connective tissue (supports and connects), glandular tissue (secretes substances — e.g. enzymes, hormones).	Science::Biology::Paper 1::Organisation
Basic	What is the role of the digestive system and name the enzymes involved?	Breaks down large insoluble food molecules into small soluble ones for absorption. Amylase: breaks down starch → simple sugars (made in salivary glands, pancreas, small intestine). Protease: proteins → amino acids (stomach, pancreas, small intestine). Lipase: lipids → fatty acids + glycerol (pancreas, small intestine).	Science::Biology::Paper 1::Organisation
Cloze	The stomach produces {{c1::hydrochloric acid}} (pH {{c2::2}}) to kill bacteria and provide optimum pH for {{c3::protease (pepsin)}}. The small intestine is neutralised by {{c4::bile}} produced in the {{c5::liver}} and stored in the {{c6::gallbladder}}.		Science::Biology::Paper 1::Organisation
Basic	How is the small intestine adapted for absorption?	Villi increase surface area. Microvilli (on villi) further increase surface area. Rich blood supply maintains concentration gradient. Thin walls (one cell thick) = short diffusion distance. Lacteals absorb fatty acids and glycerol into the lymphatic system.	Science::Biology::Paper 1::Organisation
Basic	What is the structure of the heart and how does blood flow through it?	Right side receives deoxygenated blood from body (vena cava) → pumps to lungs (pulmonary artery). Left side receives oxygenated blood from lungs (pulmonary vein) → pumps to body (aorta). Four chambers: right atrium, right ventricle, left atrium, left ventricle. Valves prevent backflow.	Science::Biology::Paper 1::Organisation
Cloze	Coronary heart disease: {{c1::fatty deposits}} build up in the coronary arteries, restricting blood flow to the heart muscle. Treated by: {{c2::statins}} (reduce cholesterol), {{c3::stents}} (keep arteries open), {{c4::bypass surgery}} (new vessel grafted around blockage).		Science::Biology::Paper 1::Organisation
Basic	What is cancer and what are the differences between benign and malignant tumours?	Cancer: uncontrolled cell division. Benign: grows in one place, doesn't spread, can be removed. Malignant: invades neighbouring tissues and can metastasise (spread through blood/lymph to other parts of the body). Risk factors: smoking, UV radiation, viral infection, genetics.	Science::Biology::Paper 1::Organisation
Cloze	Plants have: {{c1::meristem tissue}} (growth at root and shoot tips), {{c2::xylem}} (transports water and minerals UP — dead cells, lignin walls), {{c3::phloem}} (transports sugars UP and DOWN — living cells). Transpiration pulls water up through {{c4::stomata}}.		Science::Biology::Paper 1::Organisation
