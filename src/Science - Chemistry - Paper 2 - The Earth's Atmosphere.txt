Basic	What was the Earth's early atmosphere and how did it change?	Early atmosphere: mostly CO₂, water vapour, small amounts of methane, ammonia and nitrogen. Released by volcanic activity. As Earth cooled, water vapour condensed to form oceans. Oceans dissolved CO₂. Algae and plants evolved (photosynthesis) — CO₂ absorbed, O₂ released. Nitrogen built up as unreactive gas. CO₂ also locked in fossil fuels and limestone.	Science::Chemistry::Paper 2::The Earth's Atmosphere

Basic	What are the atmospheric pollutants from combustion and their effects?	CO₂: greenhouse gas → global warming. CO: toxic (binds to haemoglobin). SO₂: acid rain (dissolves in rainwater → sulfuric acid) → damages ecosystems, buildings. NO/NO₂: acid rain; formed by N₂ + O₂ at high temperatures in engines. Particulates (soot): global dimming, respiratory problems.	Science::Chemistry::Paper 2::The Earth's Atmosphere
Basic	What is the enhanced greenhouse effect and what are its consequences?	<b>Natural greenhouse effect:</b> greenhouse gases (CO₂, CH₄, H₂O vapour) absorb infrared radiation emitted by Earth's surface and re-radiate it back → keeps Earth ~33°C warmer than without atmosphere.<br><b>Enhanced greenhouse effect:</b> increased burning of fossil fuels + deforestation → higher CO₂ levels + methane from agriculture/landfill → more heat trapped → global warming.<br><b>Consequences:</b> rising sea levels (thermal expansion + ice melt), more extreme weather events, loss of habitats, species extinction, disruption to agriculture/water supplies, ocean acidification. Basic	How did the proportion of oxygen in the atmosphere increase over billions of years?	<b>Early atmosphere:</b> no oxygen — mainly CO₂, water vapour, small amounts of N₂, methane, ammonia.<br><b>Stage 1 (~3.5 billion years ago):</b> oceans formed as water vapour condensed. CO₂ dissolved in oceans (forming carbonate rocks and limestone over time).<br><b>Stage 2:</b> simple photosynthetic organisms (blue-green algae/cyanobacteria) evolved → produced O₂ by photosynthesis: CO₂ + H₂O → glucose + O₂.<br><b>Stage 3:</b> O₂ accumulated over hundreds of millions of years as organisms spread. O₂ allowed ozone layer to form → shielded from UV → more complex life could colonise land. N₂ built up as unreactive byproduct of processes.	Science::Chemistry::Paper 2::The Earth's Atmosphere
