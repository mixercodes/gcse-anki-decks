Basic	What is the difference between permanent and induced magnets?	Permanent magnets: always magnetic (e.g. bar magnet — made of steel). Induced magnets: temporarily magnetic when in a magnetic field (made of iron). Magnetic field lines run from north to south pole, closer together = stronger field.	Science::Physics::Paper 2::Magnetism and Electromagnetism
Basic	What is the motor effect and what factors affect the force?	A current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field experiences a force (the motor effect). F = BIL (force = magnetic flux density × current × length). Direction given by Fleming's Left Hand Rule: thuMb = Motion, First finger = Field, seCond finger = Current.	Science::Physics::Paper 2::Magnetism and Electromagnetism
Basic	What is electromagnetic induction?	Moving a conductor through a magnetic field (or changing the magnetic field around a conductor) induces a potential difference — and a current if the circuit is complete. The generator effect. Increasing the speed, field strength, or number of turns increases the induced EMF.	Science::Physics::Paper 2::Magnetism and Electromagnetism
