Basic	What is the difference between permanent and induced magnets?	Permanent magnets: always magnetic (e.g. bar magnet — made of steel). Induced magnets: temporarily magnetic when in a magnetic field (made of iron). Magnetic field lines run from north to south pole, closer together = stronger field. Basic	What is the motor effect and what factors affect the force?	A current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field experiences a force (the motor effect). F = BIL (force = magnetic flux density × current × length). Direction given by Fleming's Left Hand Rule: thuMb = Motion, First finger = Field, seCond finger = Current. Basic	What is electromagnetic induction?	Moving a conductor through a magnetic field (or changing the magnetic field around a conductor) induces a potential difference — and a current if the circuit is complete. The generator effect. Increasing the speed, field strength, or number of turns increases the induced EMF.	Science::Physics::Paper 2::Magnetism and Electromagnetism
Cloze	A transformer uses {{c1::electromagnetic induction}}. Step-up transformer: {{c2::more}} turns on secondary coil → higher voltage. Step-down: {{c3::fewer}} turns → lower voltage. Formula: {{c4::Vs/Vp = Ns/Np}}. Only works with {{c5::AC}} (alternating current).		Science::Physics::Paper 2::Magnetism and Electromagnetism
Basic	Transformer worked example: a step-up transformer has 500 turns on the primary and 5000 turns on the secondary. If the primary voltage is 230V, what is the secondary voltage? If the primary current is 10A, what is the secondary current (assume 100% efficiency)?	<b>Voltage:</b> Vs/Vp = Ns/Np → Vs = 230 × (5000/500) = <b>2300 V</b><br><b>Current (using P = IV, assume 100% efficient → power in = power out):</b><br>Ip × Vp = Is × Vs → 10 × 230 = Is × 2300 → Is = <b>1 A</b><br><b>Key principle:</b> step-up voltage = step-down current. Transformers conserve power (assuming no losses).	Science::Physics::Paper 2::Magnetism and Electromagnetism
Basic	What is a solenoid (electromagnet) and what factors affect its strength?	A solenoid is a coil of wire that acts as a magnet when current flows through it. The magnetic field inside is uniform and similar to a bar magnet (with N and S poles).<br><b>Factors that increase strength:</b> increase the current, increase the number of turns per unit length, add a soft iron core (greatly amplifies the field).<br><b>Advantage over permanent magnets:</b> can be switched on and off, and strength can be varied.<br><b>Uses:</b> electric bells, circuit breakers, relays, MRI scanners. Basic	What is the structure of a DC motor and how does it work?	A DC motor converts electrical energy into kinetic (rotational) energy using the motor effect.<br><b>Components:</b> rectangular coil of wire in a magnetic field, <b>commutator</b> (split-ring — reverses current direction every half turn), <b>carbon brushes</b> (conduct current to the commutator).<br><b>How it works:</b> current in coil → force (motor effect) → coil rotates. Commutator ensures current always reverses so the coil continues to rotate in the same direction rather than oscillating.<br><b>Increase speed:</b> more current, stronger magnetic field, more turns on coil. Basic	What is the difference between an AC generator and a DC generator?	Both use electromagnetic induction (moving coil in magnetic field generates EMF).<br><b>AC generator (alternator):</b> uses <b>slip rings</b> and brushes → coil connected continuously → output alternates direction as coil rotates → produces AC.<br><b>DC generator:</b> uses a <b>commutator</b> (split-ring) → reverses connection every half turn → output always in same direction → produces DC (pulsed).<br><b>Increase EMF output:</b> spin faster, use stronger magnets, use more turns on the coil. Basic	Why does the National Grid transmit electricity at high voltage? How does this reduce energy loss?	Transmitting at high voltage means the current is low (P = IV, power is constant).<br><b>Energy lost in cables = I²R</b> — power loss depends on the square of the current.<br>Halving the current → power loss reduced by a factor of 4.<br><b>Process:</b> power station generates ~25,000V → step-up transformer → 400,000V for transmission (very low current) → step-down transformers reduce to 230V for homes.<br><b>Exam tip:</b> it's the LOW CURRENT that reduces energy loss, not the high voltage directly. High voltage enables low current.	Science::Physics::Paper 2::Magnetism and Electromagnetism
Basic	How does a loudspeaker use the motor effect?	<b>Structure:</b> coil of wire attached to a paper cone, placed in the magnetic field of a permanent magnet.<br><b>How it works:</b> AC (alternating current) flows through the coil → force on coil alternates direction (motor effect) → coil moves back and forth → cone vibrates → creates compressions and rarefactions in air → produces sound waves.<br><b>Higher frequency AC</b> → coil vibrates faster → higher pitch sound.<br><b>Larger amplitude current</b> → greater force → larger vibration → louder sound. Basic	How does a microphone use electromagnetic induction?	<b>Structure:</b> diaphragm (thin membrane) attached to a coil of wire in a magnetic field.<br><b>How it works:</b> sound waves → diaphragm vibrates → coil moves relative to magnetic field → <b>EMF induced</b> (generator effect) → alternating current produced that mirrors the sound wave.<br><b>This is the reverse of a loudspeaker</b> — both use a coil in a magnetic field, but the microphone converts sound to electrical signal while the loudspeaker converts electrical signal to sound.<br><b>Exam tip:</b> microphone uses the generator effect; loudspeaker uses the motor effect.
