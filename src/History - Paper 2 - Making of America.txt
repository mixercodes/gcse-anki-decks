Basic	What were conditions like for enslaved people in the Cotton Kingdom?	The 'pushing system' forced enslaved people to farm more land each year (5 acres in 1805; 10 acres in 1850). Families were separated at slave auctions. A healthy 18-year-old male could sell for $900–$1,100 in New Orleans. Between 1815 and 1820, 2,646 children under 13 were sold in New Orleans alone. Women were searched naked to check fertility.	History::Paper 2::Making of America

Basic	What was the culture of the Plains Indians (focus on Lakota Sioux)?	Nomadic lifestyle — followed the buffalo for food, clothing, shelter (tepees). Leadership by councils of elders and chiefs; decisions made by consensus. Buffalo sacred — used for everything (food, clothing, tools, fuel). Believed land could not be owned — it belonged to everyone. Warfare was about honour (counting coup), not conquest. Clashed fundamentally with white American values. Kansas-Nebraska Act 1854 — popular sovereignty on slavery → 'Bleeding Kansas'. Republican Party founded as anti-slavery party. Lincoln elected president 1860 — South Carolina seceded immediately. Fort Sumter attacked 12 April 1861 → war declared.	History::Paper 2::Making of America

Basic	What was the experience of Black Americans during the Civil War (1861–65)?	Before 1861: in North, free but few rights; in South, enslaved. 1862: some escaped slaves used as labourers by Union army. 1863: Emancipation Proclamation freed slaves in Confederate states. Black regiments formed but initially: no equal pay; no officer roles; race riots in Northern cities. By war's end: 180,000 Black soldiers served in the Union army; gained citizenship rights in principle.	History::Paper 2::Making of America

The {{c4::14th Amendment}} (1868) granted citizenship to all people born in the USA. The {{c5::15th Amendment}} (1870) gave Black men the right to vote.		History::Paper 2::Making of America
Basic	What happened during Reconstruction (1865–77) and how limited was the progress?	Freedmen's Bureau set up to help formerly enslaved people. Land given to Black families in Sea Islands, South Carolina. Missionaries taught reading and writing. BUT: sharecropping replaced slavery economically — freed people farmed land in exchange for a share of the crop (remained in poverty). KKK used terror to stop Black people voting. By 1877, federal troops withdrew and Southern states rolled back rights.	History::Paper 2::Making of America

Basic	What was Rachel's story and why is it significant?	Rachel arrived in New Orleans in 1819. Women were searched naked to check if they could have children. In January 1819 a healthy 18-year-old male sold for $900–$1,100. Rachel was sold for $800. Her story is significant because it shows the dehumanisation of enslaved people — reduced to their physical capacity to work and reproduce — and evidence that women were sold at lower prices than men in this market.
