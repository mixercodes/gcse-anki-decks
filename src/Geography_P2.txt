Basic	What is urbanisation and what causes it?	The increase in the proportion of people living in urban areas. Caused by rural-to-urban migration (push: lack of jobs/services in rural areas; pull: jobs/education/healthcare in cities) and natural population increase.	Geography::Paper 2::Urban Issues
Basic	What is a squatter settlement and what are its characteristics?	Illegal self-built housing on land people don't own, on city outskirts in LICs/NEEs. Lack clean water, sewage, electricity, healthcare. High density, poor housing quality, high unemployment. Also called favelas (Brazil) or shanty towns.	Geography::Paper 2::Urban Issues
Basic	How can squatter settlements be improved?	Site and service schemes (government provides land, residents build). Self-help schemes (residents improve homes with support). Local authority improvements (utilities, paved roads). Redevelopment — though this may displace residents.	Geography::Paper 2::Urban Issues
Basic	What are the social, economic and environmental challenges of rapid urban growth in LIC cities?	Social: overcrowding, crime, poor healthcare/education. Economic: informal economy, unemployment, inequality. Environmental: air and water pollution, waste disposal issues, traffic congestion, lack of green space.	Geography::Paper 2::Urban Issues
Basic	What urban transport problems exist and how can they be managed?	Problems: traffic congestion, air pollution, long commutes. Solutions: bus rapid transit (BRT), metro/light rail, park and ride, congestion charging, cycling infrastructure, integrated transport systems.	Geography::Paper 2::Urban Issues

Basic	What is the Demographic Transition Model (DTM)?	Shows how birth and death rates change as a country develops. Stage 1: high BR, high DR. Stage 2: falling DR (healthcare improves). Stage 3: falling BR (education/contraception). Stage 4: low BR, low DR. Stage 5: BR may fall below DR.	Geography::Paper 2::Changing Economic World
Basic	What is HDI and what are its limitations?	Human Development Index measures development using life expectancy, education and income (GNI per capita). Limitations: ignores inequality within countries, doesn't measure happiness, freedom, gender equality or environmental quality.	Geography::Paper 2::Changing Economic World
Basic	What are the causes of uneven development?	Physical: landlocked, poor soil, extreme climate. Economic: debt, unfair trade terms, lack of investment. Historical: colonialism — resources extracted, economies distorted, infrastructure built for export not local development.	Geography::Paper 2::Changing Economic World
Basic	What is a TNC and what are its advantages and disadvantages for LICs?	Transnational corporation — operates in multiple countries (e.g. Shell, Nike). Advantages: jobs, investment, technology transfer, infrastructure. Disadvantages: profits sent to HIC HQ, low wages, poor conditions, environmental damage.	Geography::Paper 2::Changing Economic World
Basic	What are the impacts of economic development in Nigeria?	Positive: oil revenue grew economy, expanding middle class, improved infrastructure. Negative: oil pollution in Niger Delta, inequality, corruption, over-dependence on oil, environmental destruction.	Geography::Paper 2::Changing Economic World - Nigeria
Basic	What is the north-south divide in the UK and how is it being addressed?	Economic output and wealth concentrated in London and South East. North, Midlands and Wales lag behind. Solutions: Northern Powerhouse, HS2, Local Enterprise Partnerships, freeports.	Geography::Paper 2::Changing Economic World - UK
Basic	How has the UK's employment structure changed?	Primary sector (farming, mining) declined sharply. Secondary (manufacturing) declined after deindustrialisation. Tertiary (services) and quaternary (research/tech) grew significantly and now dominate.	Geography::Paper 2::Changing Economic World - UK

Basic	What is food security and what threatens it?	When all people have access to sufficient, safe and nutritious food. Threatened by: population growth, climate change, water scarcity, extreme weather, conflict, biofuels displacing food crops, food waste and unequal distribution.	Geography::Paper 2::Resource Management
Basic	What is water insecurity and what causes it?	Unreliable access to safe water. Caused by: physical water scarcity (dry climate), economic scarcity (lack of infrastructure), pollution, population growth, climate change altering rainfall, over-extraction of groundwater.	Geography::Paper 2::Resource Management
Basic	What is energy insecurity and what are its consequences?	Unreliable or inadequate access to energy. Consequences: economic (businesses can't operate), social (no heating/healthcare), political (conflicts over resources), environmental (illegal deforestation for biomass).	Geography::Paper 2::Resource Management
Basic	What are the advantages and disadvantages of renewable energy?	Advantages: no CO₂ emissions, sustainable, reduces energy insecurity. Disadvantages: intermittent (wind/solar unreliable), high upfront costs, visual impact, some habitat damage (e.g. hydroelectric dams flooding valleys).	Geography::Paper 2::Resource Management
Basic	What is the difference between large-scale and small-scale water management?	Large-scale: dams, reservoirs, water transfer schemes. Small-scale/appropriate tech: rainwater harvesting, drip irrigation, well digging, greywater recycling — more accessible for LICs and rural communities.	Geography::Paper 2::Resource Management

Geography::Paper 2::Resource Management - Chambamontera
Basic	Why was Chambamontera suited to a micro-hydro scheme?	High rainfall, steep terrain and fast-flowing rivers made water power ideal. The challenging geography (steep slopes up to 1,700m altitude, roads closed in winter) made a traditional electricity grid economically impractical.	Geography::Paper 2::Resource Management - Chambamontera
Basic	What are the social benefits of the Chambamontera micro-hydro scheme?	Students can study after dark (improved school facilities). Healthcare improved through refrigeration of medicines. Safer nighttime mobility via street lights. Reduced rural-to-urban migration — local population has grown. Less reliance on kerosene reduces fire risk.	Geography::Paper 2::Resource Management - Chambamontera
Basic	What are the economic and environmental benefits of the Chambamontera micro-hydro scheme?	Coffee processing machinery can now be used (business growth). Renewable energy with minimal maintenance costs. Negligible environmental footprint. Less wood used for heating — reduces deforestation and soil erosion. Flood risk reduced by regulating river flow. Scheme lifespan of at least 25 years.	Geography::Paper 2::Resource Management - Chambamontera
Basic	Why is Chambamontera an example of appropriate/small-scale technology?	It uses local workforce and materials. Low maintenance and running costs. Affordable for an LIC community (with credit options). Self-sufficient — the community manages it themselves. Sustainable, renewable energy source suited to the local environment. Contrasts with large-scale HIC solutions like dams or nuclear power.	Geography::Paper 2::Resource Management - Chambamontera
Cloze	The {{c1::Queen Elizabeth Olympic Park}} (Stratford, East London) was built for the {{c2::2012}} London Olympics. It regenerated one of London's most deprived areas — {{c3::Newham}}, which had {{c4::30%}} unemployment before the games.		Geography::Paper 2::Urban Issues - London
Basic	What were the social and economic benefits of the Olympic Park regeneration?	20,000 new homes planned. 8,000 permanent jobs created. New facilities: Aquatics Centre, Velodrome, London Stadium — all open to public. New schools and healthcare facilities. Stratford International station improved transport links. Westfield Shopping Centre opened — largest urban shopping centre in EU.	Geography::Paper 2::Urban Issues - London
Basic	What are the criticisms of the Olympic Park regeneration?	Original residents and businesses displaced during construction. House prices rose, pricing out local people (gentrification). Many new homes unaffordable for existing community. Some promised affordable housing targets not met. Benefits mainly attracted wealthier newcomers rather than improving lives of existing residents.
