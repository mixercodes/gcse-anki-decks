Basic	What are the four key fieldwork quality words?	Validity — conclusions answer the enquiry question. Precision — how detailed/specific the data is. Reliability — results are repeatable and consistent. Accuracy — equipment used correctly and findings reflect reality.	Geography::Paper 3::Fieldwork - Sheringham
Basic	What are the three sampling strategies?	Systematic — regular intervals (e.g. every 2m down the groyne). Random — removes unconscious bias (e.g. random number generator). Stratified — divides into sub-groups then samples randomly within each.	Geography::Paper 3::Fieldwork - Sheringham
Basic	How was groyne effectiveness measured at Sheringham?	A tape measure placed at the top of the groyne, measuring down to the top of sediment every 2m. Shows how longshore drift builds sediment on one side. Repeated on multiple groynes for reliability.	Geography::Paper 3::Fieldwork - Sheringham
Basic	How was longshore drift speed measured at Sheringham?	10m distance marked along the beach. Orange thrown into sea and timed over 10m. Speed = distance ÷ time (m/s). Repeated three times and averaged to improve reliability.	Geography::Paper 3::Fieldwork - Sheringham
Basic	How was tourism impact measured at Sheringham?	Retail diversity survey on the High Street — recording type of each shop (tourist vs local). Distance from each shop to sea wall measured by trundle wheel. Environmental Quality Survey (EQS) at midpoint of High Street.	Geography::Paper 3::Fieldwork - Sheringham
Basic	What are the limitations of Sheringham data collection?	Footsteps inaccurate for distance (step length varies). Tape measure on groyne may not be held straight. Orange drift gives point-in-time data only (tidal conditions vary). Day's weather affects wave type and LSD speed.	Geography::Paper 3::Fieldwork - Sheringham

Basic	What are the arguments for the Lincolnshire reservoir?	Addresses growing water demand in one of UK's driest areas. 175,000 new homes planned locally. Recreational facilities (sailing, cycling). Creates wetland habitats. £2.6 billion investment. Stores winter river water for summer use.	Geography::Paper 3::Issue Evaluation - Water
Basic	What are the arguments against the Lincolnshire reservoir?	50+ families lose homes. Farmers lose land and livelihoods. Construction traffic disrupts narrow rural roads. Uncertainty causes mental health stress. Critics argue leakage reduction and conservation could meet demand instead.	Geography::Paper 3::Issue Evaluation - Water
Basic	What is effective rainfall?	The rainfall remaining available for water supply after evaporation and plant uptake. Ranges from over 2,500mm/year in NW England to under 200mm in parts of eastern England — creating a supply-demand mismatch.	Geography::Paper 3::Issue Evaluation - Water
Basic	What are the causes of water pollution in the UK?	Untreated sewage discharge. Agricultural slurry and fertiliser runoff. Illegal dumping. Leaking sewers. In 2021 there were 325 category 1 and 2 incidents — a 24% increase from the previous year.	Geography::Paper 3::Issue Evaluation - Water

Basic	How do you give a six-figure grid reference?	Read eastings (left to right) first — two-digit square number then estimate tenths into the square. Then northings (bottom to top) — same process. E.g. a point 3/10 into square 52 east and 7/10 into square 34 north = 523 347.	Geography::Paper 3::Geographical Skills
Basic	What is the difference between mean, median and mode?	Mean: add all values, divide by count — can be skewed by extremes. Median: middle value when ordered — better for skewed data. Mode: most frequent value. Range: highest minus lowest.	Geography::Paper 3::Geographical Skills
