Basic	What is the structure of AQA English Language Paper 2?	Section A: Reading — two non-fiction texts (one 19th century, one modern). Q1 (4 marks): identify true statements. Q2 (8 marks): summarise differences. Q3 (12 marks): language analysis on one text. Q4 (16 marks): compare writers' methods and perspectives across both texts. Section B: Writing — Q5, viewpoint/persuasion task, 40 marks (24 content, 16 technical accuracy).	English Lang::Paper 2
Cloze	Paper 2 Q3 ({{c1::12}} marks) asks you to analyse {{c2::language}} in one text. Q4 ({{c3::16}} marks) asks you to {{c4::compare}} both texts' methods and perspectives. Q5 ({{c5::40}} marks) is {{c6::viewpoint writing}}.		English Lang::Paper 2
Basic	How should you approach Paper 2 Q2 (summarising differences)?	Identify differences in attitude, perspective or situation between the two texts. Use comparative connectives: 'whereas', 'in contrast', 'however', 'unlike'. Always quote from both texts. Don't just describe — identify the difference explicitly. Aim for 3-4 clear differences with supporting evidence from each text.	English Lang::Paper 2
Basic	How should you approach Paper 2 Q4 (comparing perspectives)?	This is the highest-mark reading question (16 marks). Compare how each writer presents their view on the topic — their methods AND their perspectives. Structure: introduce both writers' overall stance, then work through evidence from both texts side by side. Use 'whereas', 'similarly', 'in contrast'. Comment on language, tone, structure AND purpose/audience. Quote both texts in every paragraph.	English Lang::Paper 2
Basic	What does 'writers' methods' mean in Paper 2 Q4?	Methods = language choices (word choice, imagery, rhetoric), structural choices (how the text is organised, what comes first/last), tone (formal/informal, emotive/rational, humorous/serious), perspective (first person/third person, direct address), and purpose (to persuade, inform, argue, entertain). Always comment on WHY the writer made these choices — what effect do they have on the reader?	English Lang::Paper 2
Basic	What are the PERSUADER techniques for Paper 2 Q5?	Personal Pronouns (direct address — 'you', 'we'). Emotive language. Repetition and Rule of Three (tricolon). Statistics/Facts/Opinion as Fact. Use of authority ('scientists have proven'). Anecdote. Descriptive devices (similes, metaphors, adjectives, adverbs). Exaggeration/hyperbole. Rhetorical questions. Also: Synaesthesia (appeal to senses), Captatio Benevolentiae (appeal to goodwill of audience), Praeteritio (talking about something by pretending not to — 'I will not mention...').	English Lang::Paper 2
Basic	What are the forms for Paper 2 Q5 and how long should you spend?	Forms: Letter, Letter to an editor (article style), Speech, Newspaper article, Magazine article, Blog. Plan 5-10 mins. Write 30-35 mins. Check 5 mins. The form affects tone and conventions — a speech uses direct address and spoken rhythms; a newspaper article has a headline and formal tone; a blog is more conversational.	English Lang::Paper 2
Basic	What features distinguish a Grade 7-9 viewpoint response from lower grades?	Varied sentence types to affect/entertain (elongated sentences to ridicule, short sentences to shock). Patterns of language within AND across paragraphs. Extended metaphor or semantic fields used consistently. Cyclical structure — opening and closing mirror each other. Building persuasion through juxtaposition. Humorous similes. The impression that this genuinely matters to the writer.	English Lang::Paper 2
Basic	What are five ways to open a viewpoint writing piece?	1. Show the opposite side of your argument first, then undermine it. 2. Start anecdotally — a personal story that leads to your argument. 3. Pose a question that will be answered in your writing. 4. Start abstractly in a way that becomes a metaphor for your argument. 5. Be directly persuasive and emotive from the very first line.	English Lang::Paper 2
Basic	How does the model viewpoint piece about plastic use PERSUADER techniques?	Rhetorical questions: 'Do you not feel ashamed to hear...? Are you not mortified to find...?' Statistics as fact: '1 million birds and over 100,000 sea mammals die every year'. Authority figure: 'David Attenborough has affirmed...'. Rule of three: 'whole habitats...whole species...whole countries'. Emotive language: 'scourges', 'destroying', 'choking'. Anaphora: 'Simply...Merely...Only...' builds momentum.	English Lang::Paper 2

Basic	How should you approach the Paper 2 Literature unseen poetry questions?	Q1 (24 marks): How does the poet present [theme/character]? Analyse language, structure, and form. Zoom in on specific words. Comment on effect on the reader. Q2 (8 marks): Compare two poems — identify similarities AND differences in theme AND method. Use comparative connectives. Maintain balance of evidence between both poems.	English Lang::Paper 2
Basic	What is the difference between the 24-mark and 8-mark unseen poetry questions?	24-mark Q1: Detailed analysis of ONE poem — language, structure, form, effect. Similar approach to anthology analysis. 8-mark Q2: COMPARISON of both poems — focus on differences and similarities in how poets present the theme. Shorter — about 2 developed comparative points. Cloze	{{c1::Alliteration}}: first letter of words repeated. {{c2::Sibilance}}: repetition of 's' sounds. {{c3::Assonance}}: same vowel sound with different consonants. {{c4::Consonance}}: repetition of consonant sounds. {{c5::Anaphora}}: same word/phrase repeated at the start of successive lines. Basic	How do you analyse structure in an unseen poem?	Consider: number of stanzas and whether they're equal (regularity = control). Line length — long lines = flowing/overwhelming, short = fragmented/urgent. Rhyme scheme — strict = controlled/trapped, irregular = freedom/disorder. Enjambment across stanzas — ideas overflow, can't be contained. Where the volta falls — what changes? What is the first and last word/image — what does the poet foreground and end on?
