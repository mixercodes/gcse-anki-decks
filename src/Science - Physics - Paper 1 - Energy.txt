Cloze	The eight energy stores: {{c1::kinetic}}, {{c2::gravitational potential}}, {{c3::elastic potential}}, {{c4::chemical}}, {{c5::thermal}}, {{c6::magnetic}}, {{c7::electrostatic}}, {{c8::nuclear}}. Energy is conserved — it is {{c9::transferred}} between stores, never created or destroyed.		Science::Physics::Paper 1::Energy
Cloze	Energy is transferred by four pathways: {{c1::mechanically}} (by forces doing work), {{c2::electrically}} (by electric current), {{c3::by heating}} (from hot to cold), {{c4::by radiation}} (e.g. light, infrared, sound waves).		Science::Physics::Paper 1::Energy
Cloze	Kinetic energy: Ek = {{c1::½mv²}}. Where m = {{c2::mass (kg)}}, v = {{c3::speed (m/s)}}. E.g. 2kg object at 3m/s: Ek = 0.5 × 2 × 9 = {{c4::9 J}}.		Science::Physics::Paper 1::Energy
Cloze	Gravitational potential energy: Ep = {{c1::mgh}}. Where m = mass (kg), g = {{c2::9.8 m/s²}} (gravitational field strength), h = {{c3::height (m)}}. E.g. 5kg object raised 10m: Ep = 5 × 9.8 × 10 = {{c4::490 J}}.		Science::Physics::Paper 1::Energy
Cloze	Power = {{c1::energy transferred}} ÷ {{c2::time}}. Unit: {{c3::watts (W)}}. 1W = 1 J/s. A device with 60J in 3s: power = {{c4::20W}}.		Science::Physics::Paper 1::Energy
Basic	What is the difference between renewable and non-renewable energy resources?	Non-renewable: fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas) + nuclear — will run out, provide reliable baseload power, produce CO₂ (except nuclear). Renewable: solar, wind, hydroelectric, geothermal, tidal, wave, biofuel — won't run out, often intermittent, smaller environmental footprint.	Science::Physics::Paper 1::Energy
Cloze	Efficiency = {{c1::useful output energy}} ÷ {{c2::total input energy}} (× 100 for %). A device that transfers 80J usefully from 200J input: efficiency = {{c3::80/200 = 0.4 or 40%}}. Wasted energy goes to {{c4::thermal energy}} (heat).		Science::Physics::Paper 1::Energy
Basic	How is energy transferred in heating and what reduces unwanted transfer?	Conduction: vibrations passed between particles (solids). Convection: hot fluid rises, cool sinks — creates a current (fluids). Radiation: infrared electromagnetic waves — no medium needed. Reducing: insulation (traps air), double glazing, cavity walls, draught excluders, reflective foil.	Science::Physics::Paper 1::Energy
Basic	What is a Sankey diagram and how do you interpret one?	A Sankey diagram represents energy transfers. The arrow width is proportional to the amount of energy.<br><b>Reading one:</b> input energy enters from the left (total width). Useful output goes straight ahead. Wasted energy (usually heat) branches off — often downwards.<br><b>Example:</b> a filament bulb with 100J input: 10J useful light (forward arrow), 90J waste heat (downward arrow).<br><b>Efficiency from Sankey:</b> useful output ÷ total input × 100 = 10/100 × 100 = 10% efficient.<br><b>Exam tip:</b> the wasted energy arrow should be wider than the useful arrow for most devices (most real processes are <50% efficient).	Science::Physics::Paper 1::Energy
Basic	What are the advantages and disadvantages of fossil fuels as an energy resource?	<b>Advantages:</b> reliable baseload power, high energy density, existing infrastructure worldwide.<br><b>Disadvantages:</b> finite (non-renewable), burning releases CO₂ (climate change) and SO₂ (acid rain), coal mining damages landscapes.	Science::Physics::Paper 1::Energy
Basic	What are the advantages and disadvantages of nuclear power?	<b>Advantages:</b> very reliable, no CO₂ during generation, very high energy density (small fuel needed).<br><b>Disadvantages:</b> radioactive waste (difficult to dispose of safely), very expensive to build and decommission, risk of accidents (Chernobyl, Fukushima).	Science::Physics::Paper 1::Energy
Basic	What are the advantages and disadvantages of wind energy?	<b>Advantages:</b> renewable, no CO₂ during operation, cheap to run once built.<br><b>Disadvantages:</b> intermittent (only works when wind blows), visual and noise impact, potential harm to birds. Offshore is more reliable but more expensive.	Science::Physics::Paper 1::Energy
Basic	What are the advantages and disadvantages of solar energy?	<b>Advantages:</b> renewable, no CO₂ or pollution during operation, no moving parts (low maintenance).<br><b>Disadvantages:</b> intermittent (only works in daylight/clear weather), large land area required for solar farms, manufacturing panels has environmental cost.	Science::Physics::Paper 1::Energy
Basic	What are the advantages and disadvantages of hydroelectric power?	<b>Advantages:</b> reliable and renewable, no CO₂ during operation, can store energy (pump water up at off-peak times).<br><b>Disadvantages:</b> requires flooding valleys — destroys habitats and displaces communities. Limited to suitable geography.	Science::Physics::Paper 1::Energy
Basic	What are the advantages and disadvantages of biofuels?	<b>Advantages:</b> renewable, theoretically carbon-neutral (CO₂ released equals CO₂ absorbed while growing).<br><b>Disadvantages:</b> land used for fuel crops competes with food production. Growing and processing biofuels still has significant carbon footprint in practice.	Science::Physics::Paper 1::Energy
Cloze	Elastic potential energy: stored in stretched or compressed springs. Formula: Ep = {{c1::½ke²}}. k = {{c2::spring constant}} (N/m), e = {{c3::extension}} (m). A spring with k = 50 N/m stretched by 0.2 m: Ep = 0.5 × 50 × 0.04 = {{c4::1 J}}.		Science::Physics::Paper 1::Energy
