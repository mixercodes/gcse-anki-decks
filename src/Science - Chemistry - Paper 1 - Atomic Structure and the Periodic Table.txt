Cloze	An atom contains: {{c1::protons}} (positive, in nucleus), {{c2::neutrons}} (neutral, in nucleus), {{c3::electrons}} (negative, in shells). Atomic number = number of {{c4::protons}}. Mass number = number of {{c5::protons + neutrons}}.		Science::Chemistry::Paper 1::Atomic Structure and the Periodic Table
Cloze	Isotopes are atoms of the same element with the same number of {{c1::protons}} but different numbers of {{c2::neutrons}}. E.g. Carbon-12 and Carbon-14. Same chemical properties, different {{c3::mass}}.		Science::Chemistry::Paper 1::Atomic Structure and the Periodic Table
Basic	How is the periodic table organised?	Elements arranged in order of atomic number. Vertical columns = groups (elements with same number of outer electrons, similar properties). Horizontal rows = periods (elements with same number of electron shells). Metals on left, non-metals on right, separated by a zigzag line.	Science::Chemistry::Paper 1::Atomic Structure and the Periodic Table
Basic	What are the properties of Group 1 (alkali metals) and how do they change down the group?	React with water to produce hydrogen gas and a metal hydroxide (alkaline solution). React with oxygen to form metal oxides. Going down Group 1: increased reactivity (outer electron further from nucleus, easier to lose), lower melting/boiling points, less dense.	Science::Chemistry::Paper 1::Atomic Structure and the Periodic Table
Basic	What are the properties of Group 7 (halogens)?	Non-metals that exist as diatomic molecules (F₂, Cl₂, Br₂, I₂). Going down: less reactive (harder to gain an electron), higher melting/boiling points, darker colour (F=pale yellow, Cl=green, Br=orange/brown, I=grey solid/purple vapour). More reactive halogens displace less reactive ones from solutions.	Science::Chemistry::Paper 1::Atomic Structure and the Periodic Table
Basic	What are the properties of Group 0 (noble gases)?	Inert (unreactive) because they have full outer electron shells. Monatomic (exist as single atoms). Going down: higher boiling points (stronger intermolecular forces). Used in filament bulbs (Ar), balloons (He), discharge lighting (Ne/Ar).	Science::Chemistry::Paper 1::Atomic Structure and the Periodic Table

Modern model: electrons in {{c5::probability clouds/orbitals}}.		Science::Chemistry::Paper 1::Atomic Structure and the Periodic Table
