Cloze	Photosynthesis equation: {{c1::6CO₂}} + {{c2::6H₂O}} → {{c3::C₆H₁₂O₆}} + {{c4::6O₂}}. It is an {{c5::endothermic}} reaction (absorbs energy from light).		Science::Biology::Paper 1::Bioenergetics
Basic	What are the factors that limit the rate of photosynthesis?	Light intensity, CO₂ concentration, temperature, and chlorophyll concentration. Increasing any one of these increases the rate until another factor becomes limiting. At high temperatures the enzymes controlling photosynthesis denature.	Science::Biology::Paper 1::Bioenergetics
Basic	How do plants use glucose?	Respiration (energy for growth). Made into starch for storage. Made into cellulose for cell walls. Made into lipids (oils/fats) for storage. Combined with nitrates to make amino acids and proteins.	Science::Biology::Paper 1::Bioenergetics
Cloze	Aerobic respiration equation: {{c1::C₆H₁₂O₆}} + {{c2::6O₂}} → {{c3::6CO₂}} + {{c4::6H₂O}} (+ energy). Occurs in the {{c5::mitochondria}}. Releases {{c6::more}} energy than anaerobic respiration.		Science::Biology::Paper 1::Bioenergetics
Cloze	Anaerobic respiration in animals: {{c1::glucose}} → {{c2::lactic acid}} (+ small amount of energy). Causes {{c3::oxygen debt}} — lactic acid must be oxidised after exercise. In plants/yeast: glucose → {{c4::ethanol}} + {{c5::CO₂}} (fermentation).		Science::Biology::Paper 1::Bioenergetics
Basic	What happens to the body during exercise?	Heart rate, breathing rate and breath volume increase to deliver more O₂ and glucose to muscles and remove CO₂. Muscles respire aerobically then anaerobically when O₂ can't be delivered fast enough. Lactic acid builds up causing fatigue. After exercise, excess post-exercise oxygen consumption (EPOC) occurs.	Science::Biology::Paper 1::Bioenergetics
Basic	What is the response of plants to stimuli (tropisms)?	Phototropism: shoots grow towards light (positive), roots away (negative). Gravitropism: roots grow towards gravity (positive), shoots away (negative). Controlled by auxin — redistributes to shaded/lower side causing unequal growth. Gibberellins promote seed germination and stem growth. Ethene controls ripening.	Science::Biology::Paper 1::Bioenergetics
