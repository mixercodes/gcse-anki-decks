Basic	What are the key contextual influences on Jekyll and Hyde?	Victorian respectability — high society must appear honourable at all costs (only lower classes indulge). Darwin's Origin of Species (1859) — evolution suggested humans descended from primitive animals, threatening God-centred society. Deacon Brodie — respected cabinet maker who burgled at night. John Hunter — surgeon with a respectable front entrance and secret back door. Stevenson's Edinburgh — divided between respectable New Town and sordid Old Town, joined by a bridge Stevenson crossed nightly.	English Lit::Paper 1::Jekyll and Hyde
Basic	What key vocabulary should you know for Jekyll and Hyde?	Atavistic (reversion to something primitive/ancestral), Duality (composed of two parts), Duplicitous (deceitful/two-faced), Façade (deceptive outward appearance), Repression (suppressing desires so they remain unconscious), Transgressive (violating moral/social boundaries), Troglodytic (primitive, cave-dwelling character). Basic	How do doors and thresholds function as a motif in Jekyll and Hyde?	Jekyll's house has two entrances — the respectable front (Jekyll) and the sinister back door through the laboratory (Hyde). Modelled on John Hunter's house. Utterson 'haunts the door in the by-street' — obsessed with the boundary between respectable and hidden worlds. Doors represent the Victorian need to keep private desires behind closed doors.	English Lit::Paper 1::Jekyll and Hyde
Basic	How do hands function as a motif in Jekyll and Hyde?	Hands reveal true nature. Hyde's are described as 'ape-like', hairy and small — Darwinian regression made visible. Jekyll's are well-formed and respectable. When Jekyll wakes as Hyde unexpectedly, his changed hands are the first sign of loss of control. Like Macbeth's blood-stained hands, they carry the guilt of what has been done.	English Lit::Paper 1::Jekyll and Hyde
Basic	How does clothing function as a motif in Jekyll and Hyde?	Hyde wears Jekyll's oversized clothes when discovered — a diminished, hidden version inhabiting his respectable identity. Like Macbeth's 'borrowed robes', the clothes don't fit. Clothing throughout represents the Victorian façade of respectability. Basic	How do letters and documents function in Jekyll and Hyde?	The novel is entirely structured through documents — Utterson reading the will, Lanyon's letter, Jekyll's full statement at the end. Truth is only committed to writing after death — secrets that could never be spoken aloud. This reflects the Victorian need to suppress and control truth. The documentary form distances the reader from the horror, mimicking Victorian repression.	English Lit::Paper 1::Jekyll and Hyde

Basic	What is the Victorian context of Jekyll and Hyde and why does it matter?	Written in the late Victorian era when Darwin's theory of evolution had shaken the idea of God-given human superiority. Victorian society demanded absolute respectability from the upper classes. Stevenson uses the novella to expose the hypocrisy of a society that represses natural human desires — suggesting the 'respectable' Victorian gentleman is the most dangerous kind of person. Basic	How is Utterson presented as a character and what does his name suggest?	His name derives from 'utterance' — suggesting he disseminates knowledge and speaks truth; he is the reader's guide through the mystery. He is 'austere with himself; drank gin when alone, to mortify a taste for vintages' — he suppresses his own desires just as Jekyll does, but with control. Despite his righteousness, he 'haunts' streets at all hours once infected by the idea of Hyde. Stevenson uses him to show an endemic Victorian problem: professionals must protect reputation at the expense of enjoyment.	English Lit::Paper 1::Jekyll and Hyde
Basic	How is Poole used in Jekyll and Hyde?	Poole is a lens through which Stevenson shows Jekyll's depravity. He is so unsuspecting that the reader is also fooled into thinking Hyde is a separate entity using Jekyll. His acceptance of the status quo — never questioning Jekyll's immorality — shows Victorian society's blind faith in respectable, public men. Poole's loyalty ultimately helps solve the mystery when he joins Utterson to break into the cabinet.	English Lit::Paper 1::Jekyll and Hyde
Basic	What is the epistolary form of Jekyll and Hyde and why does Stevenson use it?	The novella is structured through non-fiction documents: Utterson reading the will, Lanyon's letter, Jekyll's full statement. Using letters and statements legitimises the events — for a Victorian society with an onus on facts and evidence, the horror feels real. By obscuring names and dates, Stevenson suggests it may contain true events, anonymised for those involved. The documentary form also distances the reader from the horror — mirroring Victorian repression of uncomfortable truths.	English Lit::Paper 1::Jekyll and Hyde
Basic	How does London's geography function in Jekyll and Hyde?	London's labyrinthine streets become a site of Gothic and class tension. The geographical division of the city mirrors the division of the self: respectable, wealthy areas vs squalid, immoral districts. This mirrors Stevenson's Edinburgh where he grew up: the old town housed the workforce, drinking houses and brothels; the new town was built for the wealthy and respectable. Stevenson crossed between them nightly — the novel is partly autobiographical.	English Lit::Paper 1::Jekyll and Hyde

Basic	What is the significance of Hyde's murder of Sir Danvers Carew?	The murder is described as coming 'all of a sudden' in a 'flame of anger' — the flame metaphor contains connotations of hell. Hyde's 'ape-like fury' again invokes Darwinian regression. He 'clubbed him to earth' — the noun 'earth' in terminal position evokes the funeral reading 'earth to earth, ashes to ashes'. Stevenson reminds us we are all mortal. The murder is the novel's climax — Jekyll/Hyde crosses a line that cannot be uncrossed.	English Lit::Paper 1::Jekyll and Hyde
